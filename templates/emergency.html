You are at potential Heart Attack Risk

  • Don’t smoke or use tobacco. Smoking damages your heart and blood vessels, and increases your risk of heart attack and stroke. If you smoke, quit as soon as possible. If you don’t smoke, avoid exposure to secondhand smoke.

  • Get moving. Physical activity helps you maintain a healthy weight, lower your blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar levels, and strengthen your heart. Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous aerobic exercise per week, and do some strength training at least twice a week.
